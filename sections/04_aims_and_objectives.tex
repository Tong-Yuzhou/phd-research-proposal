\section{Aims and Objectives}

This research aims to develop a computational simulation framework capable of capturing the interactive feedback between urban environments and building energy performance. Based on this mechanism, it will be possible to accurately simulate the dynamic heat exchange between cities and buildings, providing scientific support for urban planning, architectural design, and community-level energy-saving strategies.

The four research objectives are:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\arabic*)]
    \item To identify the key factors shaping the relationship between neighbourhood-scale microclimates and building energy performance;
    \item To analyse the interaction mechanisms between outdoor--indoor thermal comfort and energy consumption, with explicit consideration of human psychological and behavioural factors;
    \item To establish a bidirectional coupling framework that integrates multi-source data and enables dynamic feedback between neighbourhood microclimates and building energy performance;
    \item To validate the framework's accuracy against empirical data and explore its potential applications in urban planning and community-level energy-saving strategies.
\end{enumerate}
